================================================================================
  SPEAKER 1 — PHASE 1: THE PITCH & DASHBOARD INTRODUCTION (Time: 0:00 – 1:00)
================================================================================

[SCREEN: Open directly on the Live Dashboard Homepage / Application UI]

"Welcome to Obsyria, our final DBMS project for Team 2. 

The modern video game industry generates billions in revenue, but crucial data — 
like development budgets, engine licenses, multi-platform pricing, and user reviews — 
is usually scattered across completely different storefronts and internal records. 
This fragmentation makes it incredibly difficult to analyze market trends.

Obsyria solves this problem. What you are looking at is our presentation layer—
a centralized intelligence dashboard. But the real power lies beneath this clean UI. 
Our frontend is seamlessly integrated with a Node.js backend, which communicates 
directly with a highly-optimized, cloud-hosted MySQL database deployed on Aiven.

By utilizing secure parameterized queries from our application layer, our goal 
was to build a robust, full-stack system that safely and efficiently turns raw, 
fragmented data into actionable industry insights. I'll now pass it over to 
discuss the architectural blueprint that makes this entire data flow possible."

================================================================================
  SPEAKER 2 — PHASE 2: DATABASE ARCHITECTURE & ER DIAGRAM (Time: 1:00 – 2:00)
================================================================================

[SCREEN: Switch to the ER Diagram Slide / Image]

"To build Obsyria, we started with our Entity-Relationship design using Chen's notation. 

At the center of our architecture is the GAME entity. Surrounding it, we established 
One-to-Many relationships. For example, Developers, Publishers, and Game Engines 
each connect to Game—meaning one developer can release multiple titles.

[SCREEN: Highlight the bridge tables — GameGenre and GamePlatformListing]

For more complex data, we had to handle Many-to-Many relationships. A game can be 
listed on multiple platforms, and a platform hosts many games. We successfully 
resolved these using bridge entities—specifically GamePlatformListing and GameGenre.

[SCREEN: Highlight the Genre table]

We also implemented a recursive relationship on the Genre table. This allows a genre 
to belong to a parent genre, for example, 'Soulslike' belonging to the parent 'RPG'. 
This ER diagram served as the strict blueprint for our SQL schema."

================================================================================
  SPEAKER 3 — PHASE 3: DATABASE IMPLEMENTATION & SQL LOGIC (Time: 2:00 – 3:30)
================================================================================

[SCREEN: Switch to MySQL Workbench / Aiven Cloud Console showing setup_db.sql]

"For our implementation, we used DDL to create nine core tables, enforcing 
strict referential integrity with Primary and Foreign Keys. 

[SCREEN: Highlight the Game table definition showing Foreign Keys]

The entire schema is fully normalized to the Third Normal Form (3NF). We 
eliminated transitive dependencies—for example, Publisher Tiers are stored 
only in the Publisher table, preventing update anomalies.

[SCREEN: Scroll to the RegisterGameComplete procedure]

For DML operations, we wrote the `RegisterGameComplete` atomic transaction. 
Registering a game writes to three separate tables. Using `START TRANSACTION` 
and `ROLLBACK`, we guarantee the ACID property of Atomicity: either the entire 
insert succeeds, or nothing is saved.

[SCREEN: Highlight the GameIntelligenceSummary View]

For querying, we built dynamic Views. `GameIntelligenceSummary` uses multiple 
`INNER JOINS` to instantly combine game, developer, and engine data. We also 
used `LEFT JOINS` for DLC analysis, ensuring games with zero DLCs still appear 
in revenue reports.

[SCREEN: Highlight the ClassifyGameTier Procedure and CalculateROI function]

We also pushed heavy business logic directly to the backend. Our `ClassifyGameTier` 
Stored Procedure uses `IF-ELSEIF` logic to dynamically categorize games. For 
financials, our `CalculateROI` Scalar Function mathematically computes budget 
and revenue to return exact percentages.

[SCREEN: Show the CREATE INDEX statements]

Finally, to optimize performance, we added B-Tree Indexing on high-traffic 
columns like Title and Metacritic Score, reducing our query latency by over 
60 percent."

================================================================================
  SPEAKER 4 — PHASE 4: DASHBOARD DEEP-DIVE & OUTPUTS (Time: 3:30 – 5:00)
================================================================================

[SCREEN: Switch back to the Live Dashboard / Application UI]

"Finally, we bring all of this backend logic together into the presentation layer—
the Obsyria dashboard. The output you see here isn't just static data; it is 
dynamically generated by the advanced SQL structures we just discussed.

[SCREEN: Point to the ROI rankings on the Dashboard]

For example, take a look at these financial rankings. These Return-on-Investment 
percentages aren't hardcoded text. They are calculated on the fly by our 
`CalculateROI` scalar function every time the dashboard loads. This ensures our 
financial intelligence is always up to the millisecond.

[SCREEN: Highlight the Genre Analytics Chart]

If we look at the Genre Analytics chart, this visualization is pulling directly 
from our `GenrePerformance` view. On the SQL side, this view utilizes `GROUP BY` 
clauses and `AVG` aggregate functions. It instantly calculates the average revenue 
and Metacritic scores across dozens of titles, grouping them neatly by their 
primary genre for easy market analysis.

[SCREEN: Highlight the Top Developers Section]

Similarly, our Top Developers spotlight isn't just a simple WHERE clause. It is 
powered by a highly complex correlated subquery. The database evaluates every 
single developer row-by-row, comparing their specific game revenues against the 
dynamic average revenue of the entire database. Only those who beat the current 
market average make it to this dashboard.

[SCREEN: Final wide shot of the Dashboard]

By keeping the heavy computational lifting—the ACID transactions, complex joins, 
subqueries, and scoring algorithms—tightly secured at the database layer, the 
Obsyria frontend remains lightweight, incredibly fast, and analytically accurate. 

From 3NF normalization to B-Tree indexing, this project fully realizes a 
professional-grade intelligence system. That concludes Team 2's presentation. 
Thank you for your time."
